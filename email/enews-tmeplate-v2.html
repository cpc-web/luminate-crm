Central Park Conservancy Launches Five Borough Crew

The same techniques and management practices that make Central Park one of the most beautiful landscapes in the world will be coming to neighborhoods throughout New York City. Central Park Conservancy officially launched its Five Borough Crew: a roving team of Conservancy employees who will be sharing their expertise in 10 community parks throughout the city.

The newly assembled team of four, part of The Conservancy’s Institute for Urban Parks, is being funded in part by a $1.6 million grant from the Jerome L. Greene Foundation. The team will work side by side with Parks Department employees and community groups to improve the quality of neighborhood parks. The Foundation hopes its gift will inspire other donors to pitch in to fund the Conservancy’s mission of helping other parks.

Visit our blog to read more about the crew and their work last week at Fort Greene Park, a 30 acre site with a rich history as Brooklyn’s first public park.

MAURICIO PERDOMO, SENIOR ZONE GARDENER

Mauricio has worked for Central Park Conservancy for 16 years. When asked to describe what he does, he says he is much more than just a gardener. “I am also a maintenance person and tour guide, “ he says. “The tourists they come with their Wi Fi, their GPS, their internet and even their maps, but when they set foot in the park they are lost. So they come to me and I like to help them.” Mauricio’s zone covers the area from 58th street to 65th street but he says he knows every inch of the park. ”You could send me out right now with my eyes closed and I will go exactly where you want me to go.”
